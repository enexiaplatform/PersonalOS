# Zettelkasten note system

The Zettelkasten method is a personal knowledge management system that involves creating a network of interconnected notes, called 'zettels', to store and organize information. It was originally developed by German sociologist Niklas Luhmann. The system is based on the idea of creating a web of notes that are linked together through keywords, tags, and cross-references. Each note, or 'zettel', is a self-contained unit of information that can be a sentence, a paragraph, or a page. The notes are then linked together to form a network of relationships between ideas, concepts, and information. The Zettelkasten method is designed to facilitate the creation of new ideas, the organization of knowledge, and the development of a personal knowledge base. It can be used for a variety of purposes, including research, writing, and learning.
